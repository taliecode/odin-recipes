Chocolate chip brownie recipe

    Imagine sinking your teeth into a warm, fudgy chocolate brownie with a rich, melt-in-your-mouth texture. The top is slightly crackly, and the inside is dense and moist, bursting with deep chocolate flavor. As you bite in, the chocolate chips scattered throughout add bursts of gooey chocolate goodness, making every bite even more indulgent. The perfect balance of sweetness and chocolatey bitterness will leave you craving more.

    Heres a delicious and easy chocolate brownie recipe to recreate this perfect treat

    Ingredients

    • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, melted
    • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
    • 2 large eggs
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1/3 cup (40g) unsweetened cocoa powder
    • 1/2 cup (65g) all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
    • 1/2 cup (90g) chocolate chips (optional)

    Steps

    1. Preheat the Oven
      • Set your oven to 350°F (175°C).
      • Grease an 8x8-inch square baking pan, or line it with parchment paper for easy removal later.
    2. Mix the Wet Ingredients
      • In a medium bowl, melt the butter in the microwave or on the stove.
      • Once melted, whisk in the sugar until smooth and well combined.
      • Add the eggs one at a time, beating after each addition. Then stir in the vanilla extract.
    3. Add the Dry Ingredients
      • In a separate bowl, sift together the cocoa powder, flour, salt, and baking powder.
      • Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the wet mixture, stirring until everything is just combined (don’t overmix).
    4. Add Chocolate Chips (Optional)
      • If you want extra chocolatey goodness, fold in chocolate chips or chunks.
    5. Transfer to the Pan
      • Pour the brownie batter into your prepared pan and use a spatula to spread it evenly.
    6. Bake
      • Bake the brownies in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes. Start checking around 20 minutes.
      • Insert a toothpick into the center—if it comes out with a few moist crumbs (not wet batter), the brownies are done.
    7. Cool and Serve
      • Let the brownies cool in the pan for at least 10 minutes before cutting into squares.
      • Serve warm or at room temperature for the perfect chewy, fudgy texture.
